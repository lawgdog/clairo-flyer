How it works — staying self-directed
You stay in self-directed. We become your provider through your FMCS — and we take it from there.
  • Reach out to us — we’ll walk you through your options and make sure this is the right fit for your family
  • Clairo Care becomes your provider through your FMCS. Your support broker coordinates the switch — no waiver changes needed
  • We become your caregiver’s employer of record — we handle payroll, background checks, required trainings, and compliance
  • Caregiver completes onboarding online — background check, CPR/first aid upload, and 6–8 hrs of self-paced trainings. Usually about one week total
  • Services begin — your caregiver submits hours and mileage through our simple app. Weekly paychecks, automatic. No invoices or timesheets on your end. EVV-exempt families log hours directly — no clock-in required.
ⓘ Want to take even more off your plate? Switching to our traditional agency model removes the FMCS entirely. Ask Clairo about this option.
The Difficulty of Care tax exemption — you keep it
🔒 If you have the exemption now, you will keep it with us
If your family caregiver currently receives the Difficulty of Care tax exemption — meaning their wages are not subject to federal or state income tax because they live with the person they support — that does not change when you come to Clairo Care. We process the exemption directly through our payroll system and set it up during onboarding so it is in place from day one, exactly as it is now.

Why we’re able to pay more than other agencies
We’re a different kind of agency — and the math is simple.

We use technology to keep our overhead low. Most agencies carry high administrative costs — large offices, big staffs, outdated systems. We run lean using modern tools, which means more of every Medicaid dollar flows directly to caregivers instead of getting absorbed by operations.
